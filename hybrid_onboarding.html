Step 3 — Set your baseline
Benchmark Tests

Our key sessions are calibrated to your fitness level using these performance benchmarks. If you haven't done them recently, complete them before you begin — they ensure you're training at the right intensity every time.

Cardio
10K Run Total Time
Max-effort 10K, ideally a recent race PR. Every run in the program — intervals, tempo, long runs — is paced from this number. The more accurate it is, the better your training.
2K Row Total Time
Row 2,000m at max effort. Sets your target paces for all rowing sessions — from long aerobic rows to threshold and VO₂ max intervals.
2K Ski Erg Total Time
Ski 2,000m at max effort. Same purpose as the 2K Row — defines your ski pacing across all intensities throughout the program.
Karen Total Time
150 wall balls for time. A classic conditioning benchmark that tests muscular endurance and pacing. Wall ball progressions in the program are calibrated to your score here.
Strength
1RM Back Squat One Rep Max
Your heaviest single back squat. Used to prescribe loading percentages for all squat-based strength work.
1RM Strict Press One Rep Max
Your heaviest single strict press. Sets the baseline for all overhead pressing progressions.
1RM Deadlift One Rep Max
Your heaviest single deadlift. Used to guide loading on all hinge-pattern strength sessions.
Max Pull-Ups Max Reps
Maximum unbroken pull-ups. Used to scale pull-up volume and intensity throughout the program.
Step 4 — Speak the language
Acronym & Glossary
1RM
One Rep Max
Your absolute heaviest single lift — the maximum weight you can move for one clean, complete repetition.
AHAP
As Heavy As Possible
Use your heaviest weight that still allows clean, safe reps throughout the full set.
AMRAP
As Many Reps As Possible
Keep moving at a steady pace until the timer ends. Your score is total reps or rounds completed.
BBJ
Burpee Box Jump
Perform a burpee, then jump up onto the box. Step or jump back down to repeat.
BBJO
Burpee Box Jump Over
Perform a burpee, then jump over the box laterally or facing forward.
BJO
Box Jump Over
Jump onto and over the box — no burpee. Land on the other side and repeat.
DBall
Slam Ball / Dead Ball
A heavy, non-bouncing rubber ball used for slams, carries, and over-the-shoulder movements.
EMOM
Every Minute on the Minute
Start the prescribed work at the top of every minute. Rest for whatever time remains before the next minute begins.
FFE
Front Foot Elevated
Elevating the front foot on a weight plate to increase range of motion and quad loading on lunge-pattern movements.
FR
Front Rack
Holding a barbell, DB, or KB at shoulder height in the front rack position.
G2OH
Ground-to-Overhead
Moving a weight from the floor to fully locked out overhead in one fluid motion.
OTO
Over The Object
Jumping laterally or linearly over a dumbbell or sandbag — often performed after completing a burpee.
RFT
Rounds For Time
Complete all prescribed rounds as fast as possible. Your score is your total time.
ROM
Range of Motion
Full range of motion is expected on all movements unless the workout notes specify otherwise.
RPE
Rate of Perceived Effort
A 1–10 scale to gauge intensity. RPE 7 means a few reps left in the tank; RPE 10 is an all-out max effort.
SB
Sandbag
A soft, loadable bag used for carries, cleans, and ground-to-shoulder movements.
Super Set
Back-to-Back Exercises
Two or more exercises performed back-to-back with zero rest between them. Rest only after the last movement is done.
TC
Time Cap
A time limit on the workout. Stop where you are when the cap hits and record reps or rounds completed.
WB
Wall Ball Shot
Squat holding a medicine ball, then drive up explosively and throw the ball to hit a target on the wall.
Ubk/Unbk
Unbroken
Completing a target set of repetitions continuously without pausing, dropping the implement, or breaking your athletic posture.
Got questions?
FAQ
I'm short on time — which track and sessions should I prioritise? +
Start with the FLEX track and skip Optional blocks when needed. If 5 days is still too much and you want to compete in hybrid races, prioritise running (Thursday and Sunday) and conditioning (Tuesday and Friday) — those four sessions cover the core of the program.
FLEX feels like too little volume, but I can't commit to double sessions. What should I do? +
Follow the PRO track but focus on the AM sessions, treating PM blocks as optional. You'll get the extra volume without the time pressure of double session days. However, if you manage to squeeze in some doubles, running sessions should be your priority.
What if I don't have a specific piece of equipment? +
Most days have an alternative session (e.g. A1 vs A2) or a substitution noted in the Notes section. If neither applies, ask in the WhatsApp group — we'll sort something out. Just don't skip the session entirely.
I'm new to running. Should I reduce volume? +
Probably yes. Start with the FLEX track (2 runs/week) and keep your weekly mileage close to what you're already used to — cap the long runs if needed to stay within that range. From there, build progressively: no more than 5–7% increase per week. Drop us a message if you need help adjusting volume to fit your routine.
I don't know my 1RM lifts. Should I test them all before starting? +
Not necessary — a solid estimate is enough to work at the right intensities, since raw strength maxes aren't the main progression metric in this program. If you have no reference at all and we're not in a testing week, reach out and we'll help you fit those tests into your schedule.
I don't have recent running or erg benchmarks. Does that matter? +
Yes — much more than the strength lifts. Running and erg paces drive a large part of the program and define the right intensity for those sessions, so accuracy really matters, especially for running. A recent 10K race time (within the last 6–8 months) is ideal. If you've never tested the 2K Row or 2K Ski, ask us how to fit them into your calendar from the start.
How are paces calculated? +
Run paces are based on your 10K benchmark; row and ski paces on your 2K tests. Enter your results in the app and all session targets are set automatically. From there, paces are calibrated to the goal of each session — easy, threshold, VO₂ max — so every athlete does the same workout but each one trains at the right intensity for their fitness level.
What score do I log if I don't finish within the time cap? +
Log the time cap as your score and note in the comments where you stopped. Don't keep going past the cap — it defeats the purpose. The cap is there to prompt you to scale (reps, weight, or distance) so you can finish within it next time.
Can I swap or reorder training days? +
Yes, but think it through. The program is built so muscle groups recover while others work. Occasional swaps are fine — but doing two heavy leg days or two high-volume conditioning sessions back-to-back will generate more fatigue than expected and leave you feeling more worn out than usual.
